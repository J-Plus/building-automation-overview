### NEW YORK — KEY MARKET DRIVER: Local Law 97

NYC Local Law 97 (Climate Mobilization Act, 2019) is the **single biggest demand driver** for BAS work in New York right now:

| Metric | Data |
|---|---|
| **Buildings affected** | Most buildings over 25,000 sq ft (~50,000 buildings) |
| **Compliance deadline** | Emissions limits began 2024; stricter limits in 2030 |
| **Estimated job creation** | Up to **141,000 local jobs** by 2030 (Urban Green Council) |
| **Retrofit market size** | Potential **$20 billion** in economic activity (13x current retrofit market) |
| **Retrofit projects** | ~25,000 projects in ~5,500 buildings for prewar multifamily alone by 2030 |
| **Penalty for non-compliance** | $268 per metric ton of CO2 over the limit, annually |
| **What it means for BAS** | Every affected building needs energy audits, BAS upgrades/optimization, metering, and ongoing monitoring to meet emission caps |

> **Bottom line:** LL97 is creating an unprecedented wave of BAS retrofit work across tens of thousands of NYC buildings, driving demand for technicians who can install, program, and optimize building controls for carbon reduction. Companies specializing in LL97 compliance + BAS are already in high demand.
