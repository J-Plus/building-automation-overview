# Building Automation Labor & Workforce Data

## The Shortage at a Glance

- **550,000+** unfilled construction positions (2026)
- **480,000+** unfilled HVAC jobs specifically
- HVAC technician shortage expected to reach **225,000** by 2025
- **42,500** average annual HVAC job openings, many remaining unfilled

## BAS Technician Specialization

- BAS technicians are a specialized subset of HVAC/controls professionals
- Modern BAS work requires **data analysis** skills, not just mechanical intuition
- Adjusting airflows/energy loads involves interpreting **networked control logic**
- Employers increasingly seek technicians with skills in:
  - Energy auditing
  - Building automation systems
  - Indoor air quality
  - Smart building technologies

## Training & Retention Data

- Companies prioritizing technician training see **24% higher profit margins**
- **30-50% improvement** in retention rates
- Service call times shorten by **20%**
- Customer satisfaction increases by **15%**
- Modern training: modular 6-week intensive courses covering health, safety, air quality, and controls

## The Implication for Market Growth

The labor shortage is both a **constraint** and a **driver** for the BAS market:
- **Constraint:** Lack of qualified technicians slows deployment and maintenance
- **Driver:** Labor scarcity accelerates adoption of automation, remote monitoring, and AI-driven fault detection to do more with fewer people
